The Reality

Most ghee sold in India today is made from frozen cream, processed in large factories, and blended with refined oils. The 'pure' label means nothing without knowing the cow breed, the process, and the farmer.

What's Missing

Why it takes 3 days

Every shortcut in ghee-making costs you something.

1

Morning milk

Gir cows are milked at sunrise. A2 milk only — never mixed with other breeds or frozen stock.

2

Cultured into curd

Milk is set as curd with a live culture. This is what most factory ghee skips entirely.

3

Hand-churned butter

Curd is churned by hand using a traditional wooden churner. Slow. Inefficient. Essential.

4

Slow-cooked ghee

Butter is simmered on low heat until perfect. Watched. Adjusted. Never rushed. Never automated.
